# Research Task Map

## What You Are Trying To Do

You are developing an HCI research project about how people should command and understand teams of AI agents.

The central move is to replace the mental model of "chatting with one assistant" or "watching many agents talk" with a command interface based on formations.

## Near-Term Goal

Turn the idea into a defensible research proposal:

1. Define the problem clearly.
2. Show why existing conversational, agent-chat, and workflow interfaces are insufficient.
3. Introduce formation as a new interaction unit.
4. Build a prototype that makes the concept concrete.
5. Design a study that can evaluate whether this interface helps users.

## Literature Areas To Map

- Mixed-initiative interaction.
- Supervisory control and human-in-the-loop systems.
- Human-swarm interaction and command interfaces.
- Multi-agent LLM systems and agent orchestration.
- Explainable AI and transparency for collaborative AI.
- End-user programming, workflow automation, and visual orchestration.
- HCI design prototypes for AI systems.

## Research Gap Candidate

Existing systems often optimize agent autonomy or task performance, but give less attention to the user's command experience: how users choose collaboration modes, interpret group state, intervene in time, and recover control when the agent team drifts.

## Evaluation Questions

- Do formations help users understand what a multi-agent system is currently doing?
- Do formations reduce orchestration effort compared with prompt-only control?
- Do formations improve the user's sense of control and ability to intervene?
- Can users correctly predict what will happen after switching formations?
- Which formation concepts are intuitive, and which create confusion?
